## Harmonic and melodic techniques
- [[Dorian mode]]
	- [[Scale]] with [[Scale|a raised 6th]]
- Chromatic mediants
	- [[Chords|chord changes]] that move by a third
	- (C major to E major)
	- creates a sudden "magical" or "otherworldly" feeling
- Arpeggiation
	- harp-like, rapid, arpeggiated chords
	- fast paced arpeggiation on a pulse wave makes a magical, shimmering atmosphere
- Suspended Chords & Quartal Harmony
	- instead of simple major/minor chords
	- use "sus" chords, or [[Building chords on xths|chords built on fourths]] to create a "suspended" or "ancient" atmosphere

## Instrument and Sound Design
- Square waves (Lead)
	- Use 25% or 50% [[pulse widths]] for melodic leads
	- Soften the square wave with a low-pass filter or subtle delay to mimic wind instruments
- Triangle Wave (Bass/Strings)
	- Provides a soft, warm bass or cello-like sound to [[ground the melody]]
- Noise Wave (Percussion)
	- Noise channels for dramatic [[timpani rolls]]
	- sweeping wind sounds
	- or foggy effect
- Rapid arpeggiation effects
	- Simulate harp or lute like textures with rapid arps that move between notes within the chord

## Arrangement & Composition
- Active Bassline
	- [[What makes something melodic|Make the bassline melodic]] rather than just playing root notes
	- should feel like a [[supporting voice]]
- Layering
	- [[Layering|Layer]] a pulse for clarity with a triangle wave an octave lower to add warmth, mimicking a duet
- Melody style
	- Keep melodies catchy and simple
	- mimick earworm melodies of early NES soundtracks
- Dynamics
	- start with a simple quiet melody using a triangle wave
	- gradually introduce a brighter square wave lead for a crescendo effect
	- simulates a growing adventure
