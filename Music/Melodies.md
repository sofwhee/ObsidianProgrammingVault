
### Counter melody

- Often moves when main melody holds long notes
- Uses different, complementary rhythms to avoid blending into simple harmony
- Designed to sound harmonious with the primary melody, but remaining distinct
- remains in the background, in a different register or diff intstrument

Common techniques
- Call and response: counter acts as "answer" to "question" or phrase posed by the main melody
- Contrapuntal movement: Moves in contrast to main one, like going own when main goes up
- imitation - repeats or imitates a motive previously stated by the main melody

example
- a woodwind section playing a new melody under the main string theme
