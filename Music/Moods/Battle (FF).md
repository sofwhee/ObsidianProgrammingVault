Phrygian Mode

![[Pasted image 20260513130113.png]]
You take the minor scale, and you make the 2nd note flat. (The eye of disgu)

You take these notes -> Make a series of chords -> The chords make up a key

Basic chords are triads
major minor dimished augmented

To make the first chord, take the first note "E" and then skip a note which is G. 
Then skip from G which takes you to B.

Repeat with each note.

(remember, roman numerals are upper case when major, and other such notations.)

i = Em: (E minor) = EGB
II = F (F Major) = FAC
And so on...
![[Pasted image 20260513131759.png]]
i diminished - II - II
These are the Diatonic Chords in the E Phrygian key.

That is to say, chords that are made *purely* from the notes in the E Phrygian scale.

If you want to emphasize this is the *Phrygian* key and not the Aeolian key, keep coming back to the diminshed F with your chord choices.

i II i vii - kinda sinister, struggle, villainous
i II III II - from low numbers to high numbers is a rising escalator (the more you do it, the less impact it has)
i III vii II - sounds kinda sad
i iv III II - sounds mournful and tragic

v dim and VI
Diminished chords have an unsettled tension

Inversions
they're used to add interesting basslines to your chord progression
lowest note = root (where the chord letter comes from)
if you make other note the lowest = inversion
2 possible inversions in each triad
bass note is the letter after the "/"

In the second half they start with the diminished V chord because it uses the same bass note as the 2nd inversion "i" chord at the start, so it doesn't sound out of place.
They create a descending feel in the bassline in the second half of this.
