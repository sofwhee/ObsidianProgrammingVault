Minor keys:
- Struggle, lamentation, uneasiness

Major keys:
- Triumph
- A major (optimist, trust)
- Bb (joyful, hopeful)

Hope within sadness:
- [[minor key but highlight a major chord]]. 
	- Change nautral minor chord to a V chord (major)
	- Raise the 3rd of the dominant chord (the 7th scale degree) by a semitone
	- In A minor, change E G B (Em) to E G# B (E major)
	- The raised 7th (leading tone) creates a strong tension that pulls back to the minor tonic
- playing a G minor key and lingering on a Bb major chord

Tension/despair
- Dissonant chords
- diminished intervals
- or tritones
- start with Lower, more subdued tone. Then make melody higher and volume louder, like when a person talks when they are getting angry
- release tension by lowering volume or takin instruments away to allows for reflection, or resolving dissonant chord into a major one

Tempo:
- 20 bpm increments make a significant difference in mood
- Relaxation/sadness - slower tempos (around 60 bpm)
- Excitement/anxiety - 120+ bpm and faster, more urgent rhythms
- Uncertainty/Introspection - syncopations or offbeat rhythms

Instrumentation:
- Intimacy: start with a sparse arrangement, like single piano, generally adding layers
- Anger/Power: Harsh or distorted sounds, heavy bass, energetic instruments like electric guitars
- Nostalgia/Ambience: Reverb, echo, vintage instrument samples to evoke a sense of memory
