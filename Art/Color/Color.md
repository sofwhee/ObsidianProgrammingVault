## Cool Light

in real life, you almost never see base color. You only see base color as affected by the light.

Remember the force idea?
Coming from the saturation level of the base color?

Well, in this case, the saturation of the object is much stronger than the light. So the saturation resists the light better. It's not moving very far. It still retains its redness.

![[Pasted image 20260424103352.png]]

The ambient light is the same. Why? 
The light has the most impact on the light family, not the shadow family.

You only need the light family to trend cooler than the shadow family in this case. So if it's accomplishing that without much change, you're good.
