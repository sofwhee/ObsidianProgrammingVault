(Really Simple Syndication)

An XML file format.
It allows users to automatically receive updates from websites.
Examples : blogs, news sites, podcasts

Why? So you don't have to visit the website to see new updates.
It centralizes information.

It's a curated stream of new content.
It includes headlines, summaries, publication date, and links to the original.
These are delivered to an RSS reader (aggregator).

#### Usage:

Need a reader. (Feedly, Inoreader, browser extensions)
Then, you can subscribe to the website's RSS feed.
Subscribe by 
- clicking on the RSS icon, or subscribe button.
- or, pasting the link in the reader
The reader will notify you of new content automatically.
